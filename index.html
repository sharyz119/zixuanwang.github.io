Biography

I am a researcher standing at the intersection of Artificial Intelligence, Reliability, and Human-Centric Computing. I've completed my MSc in AI for Health at Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam. My research focuses on developing trustworthy and interpretable AI systems for healthcare applications.

My Research

I specialize in Reinforcement Learning and Uncertainty Quantification. I was driven by a desire to build AI that knows what it doesn't know. My thesis work investigates the correlation between epistemic and aleatoric uncertainty in offline RL, applying calibration and conformal prediction to achieve prediction intervals with better interpretability. I believe that AI systems should not just "wing it" ‚Äî they should provide guarantees that deserve our trust.

My research interests include:

  • Offline Reinforcement Learning
  • Uncertainty Quantification & Calibration
  • Medical AI & Clinical Decision Support
  • Multimodal Learning & Image Fusion
  • Trustworthy & Explainable AI

Background

I hold dual master's degrees: one from VU Amsterdam (AI for Health, thesis grade 9.0/10.0) and one from the University of Electronic Science and Technology of China (UESTC). Previously, I earned my Bachelor's in Communication Engineering, where I was awarded the National Scholarship (Top 0.2%).
